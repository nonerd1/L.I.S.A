Design Iteration Gallery

September 2022

Initial Concept Sketch

First conceptual drawing of telescopic mechanism. The idea was to use a rack and pinion system initially.

October 2022

First CAD Model

First 3D model in Fusion360. We quickly realized the rack and pinion would be too heavy.

November 2022

Prototype v1

First 3D printed prototype. Failed due to insufficient wall thickness and weak attachment points.

December 2022

Linear Actuator Integration

Switched to linear actuators for extension mechanism. Required significant redesign of the arm structure.

January 2023

Prototype v2

Second prototype with reinforced joints and actuator mounting points. Material thickness increased.

February 2023

Prototype v3

Third prototype added guide rails to prevent arm wobble when extended. Much more stable.

February 2023

Motor Mount Design

Redesigned motor mount to integrate with telescopic arm. Improved weight distribution.

March 2023

Final Arm Design

Final arm design with optimized weight, proper actuator housing, and reinforced extension rails.

April 2023

Integration Testing

Testing the fully integrated arm with electronics. Some issues with wire routing were identified and fixed.

Design Challenges

"Yes, we literally had to put our arms back on after they fell off."

The most challenging aspect of the design process was balancing weight, strength, and functionality. Early prototypes suffered from weak connection points that would break during flight testing. We solved this by reinforcing key stress points and using a higher infill percentage for 3D printing.

Weight Optimization

Each design iteration reduced weight while maintaining structural integrity. Final version is 40% lighter than our first prototype.

Arm Stability

Added guide rails and bearing surfaces to eliminate wobble when arms are extended. Critical for maintaining flight stability.

Actuator Integration

Designed custom housing for linear actuators with proper heat dissipation and protection from crash damage.
